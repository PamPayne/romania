## Operational Requirements

The list below describes the high level operational requirements which would need to be fulfilled according to Romania regulation. The list is a guideline and shall not be understood as an exhaustive list which guarantees compliance.

### Registration

A compliant registration solution will need to be developed.

Only a single player account is permitted.

### External Register Checks

There is no external register solution available in Romania, so external register checks are not required.

### Player Journey

A full range of player statuses with associated actions will need to be provided. An update will need to be developed to support an Access Ban of up to seven days.

### Banking

A compliant banking solution will need to be developed, which includes limitations on deposits and withdrawals for Provisional Players, and support of weekly and monthly deposit limits.

Transactions may only be conducted through payment processors approved by the National Gambling Office. Players must have bank accounts in Romania.

### Backoffice updates

Backoffice tools will need to be updated to support Romania requirements including but not limited to:

- Help Desk
- Financial Transactions (limits)
- Update Account Status
- Document verification
- Remove exclusion
- Support of new Responsible Gaming requirements
- Reporting – statuses, limits, any new Responsible Gaming requirements and bespoke reports required by the National Gambling Office

### Session Reminders

Session reminders must be available as a reality check, to assist players to keep track of the time they spend gambling.

### Incomplete Games

Games must be completed before players can play another game.

Microgaming provides an Incomplete Games Sweeper. If players leave a bet open for more than 90 days, the sweeper job cancels the bet and returns the bet to the player.

Note:

The operator will be required to advise players of this on your Terms and Conditions page.

### Currencies

The default currency for Romania is the Romanian Leu. The Leu currency symbol is not supported and does not display in the games.

For example, one thousand Leu is displayed as 1,000.00.

The operator will be required to advise players of this on the Terms and Conditions page.

### Languages

The default language in Romanian casinos is English.

### Terms and Conditions

It is the operator’s responsibility to manage the Terms and Conditions page for their casino.

## Player Protection Content

- General protection  
  Stipulated player protection information must be accessible to players on the casino website. The content aims to assist players in gaming responsibly, and must include:
  - RTP’s for all available games in the casinos
  - The harmful effects of gambling
  - Protection of Minors information
  - Gambling addiction call centre details
  - Third party websites such as Gamblers Anonymous
  - The casino’s privacy policy
  - The casino’s terms and conditions
  - Responsible gaming tools and their descriptions
  - How to file a claim or complaint against the casino
  - A gambling addiction self-test

In addition to this the following Casino Information must be displayed:

- The gambling organiser’s name
- The registration number
- Tax identification data and/or another identification number
- Address of registered office
- Series, number and date of the license to operate
- Validity period of license
- Link to the website of the National Gambling Office (www.onjn.gov.ro)

## Responsible Gaming & Self Restriction Requirements

To meet regulatory requirements, self-restriction options must:

- Be available to players
- Be managed in accordance with the relevant regulations
- Be enforced by you when players exceed their limits

Loosening of restrictions must have a cooling period of 48 hours before being applied.

Tightening of restrictions should be applied instantly and no later than 24 hours.

The stipulated self-restrictions include:

- Exclusions - temporary and permanent plus access ban of up to 7 days.
- Weekly and Monthly Deposit Limits

## Backup and Mirror Server

The casino system must consist of a central computer system, a terminal located at the National Gambling Office and a mirror centralised computer system, for organisers whose central computer system is not located in Romania, consisting of a back-up server and a mirror server located at premises that can be inspected by authorised National Gambling Office representatives.

Morpho provide a solution for the backup and mirror server, so a CTQ type structure and integration with Morpho is an option. Alternatively, a mirror server will need to be provided in Romania

## Reporting & SAFE Requirements

The National Gambling Office requires numerous bespoke daily reporting solutions via the terminal located at the National Gambling Office that will need to be developed.

A solution will need to be developed to support this. Morpho provide a solution for this, so integration with Morpho is an option.

### Game Type Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Game Type Report (or a report with a similar name) for the specified period and at least for the month to date (MTD), the year to date (YTD) and the life to date (LTD) corresponding to each individual game (e.g. payable).

- The period selected
- The type of game
- The total number of game participants
- The total bets
- The total winnings
- The total sum of money added to progressive jackpots, if applicable
- The total reimbursements
- The organiser's earnings
- The total funds corresponding to incomplete games

### Game Profit Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Game Profit Report (or a report with a similar name) for the specified period of the report and at least for the month to date (MTD), the year to date (YTD) and the life to date (LTD).

- The total sums of money deposited in the player's accounts
- The total sums of money withdrawn from the player's account
- The total sums of money currently available in the player's accounts
- The player's earnings

### Game Session Report

A report also submitted to the mirror server must contain info on each gaming session. A gaming session for betting games will start when a player registers as a game participant and ends when the game winner has been declared. For the other types of games of chance, the session will start when the player registers as a game participant and will end when he/she leaves the game.

- The type of game
- The unique ID of the gaming session
- The unique ID of the player
- The time when the session started
- The time when the session ended
- Relevant geolocation information
- The sum of money bet during the session
- The sum of money won during the session
- The sum of money lost during the session
- The organiser's earnings
- Any promotions or bonuses received during the session
- Any promotions or bonuses bet during the session
- The funds deposited into the player's authorised account during the session
- The funds withdrawn from the player's authorised account during the session
- The reason for suspending the session
- The game account balance at the start of the session
- The game account balance at the end of the session
- The remaining funds, for incomplete games

### Jackpot Configuration Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Jackpot Configuration Report (or a report with a similar name) for each progressive jackpot on the website, for the specified period of the report.

- The name of the progressive jackpot;
- The start date and time of the progressive jackpot;
- The configuration parameters (sum of money per day, prize percentage, etc.) of all primary and secondary jackpots (including any redirected sums of money);
- The unique element of the list of payouts for each existing game;
- The total amount of the bets that are eligible for the progressive jackpot(s);
- The total amount of the progressive jackpots won;
- The total contributions to the jackpot won;
- The start of the jackpot or other values that are not financed from contributions;
- The current amount of each jackpot prize offered as part of the respective jackpot;
- The current amount of redirected jackpot contributions;
- The end date and time of the jackpot;
- The limit value of the progressive jackpot, if applicable;
- The sum of money by which this limit is exceeded, if applicable.

### Jackpot Winnings Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Jackpot Winnings Report (or a report with a similar name) for each progressive jackpot on the website, for the specified period of the report.

- The name of the jackpot
- The unique identifier of the list of game payouts
- The unique ID of the gaming session;
- The unique ID of the player
- The ID of the game cycle
- The date and time of the jackpot prize
- The maximum jackpot level
- The amount of the jackpot
- The user's ID and the name of the employee who processes the winnings, if applicable
- The user's ID and the name of the supervisor who confirms the winnings, if applicable

### Significant Events Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply a Significant Event Report.

- The system's failed attempts to connect to the gaming platform
- The periods in which the remote gaming system or any component of the remote gaming system was unavailable (e.g. when a transaction cannot be carried out)
- Statistical reports, for selectable periods of time, on the operation of the central computer system, the back-up server, the mirror server and the terminal located at ONJN
- Winnings which exceed EUR 15 000
- All transfers of funds (simple and correlated for a definite period of time) which exceed EUR 15 000
- Any corrections made
- The compulsory deactivation of an unauthorised player
- Any other activity that required the intervention of an employee and took place outside of the normal operating scope of the system
- Other significant or unusual events

### Change Notification Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply a Change Notification Report (or a report with a similar name) for all the changes to the configuration or parameters of the system, game or event.

Licensed companies must supply a report comparing the previous settings to the new settings of the game or event.

- The method used to audit/check the information exchanged/modified by the administration accounts
- All date/time changes made on the main server
- Any parameter changes
- Identification of the employee who made the changes to the game parameters (e.g. game rules, payout programmes, rake percentage, etc.)

### Exclusion Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply an Exclusion Report (or a report with a similar name) for all players who are excluded or self-excluded.

- The unique ID of the player
- The type of exclusion (permanent, self-exclusion, etc.)
- The start date of the exclusion
- The end date of the exclusion, if applicable
- The reason for exclusion
- How many times the player has been excluded (on the date of the report)

### Account Balance Adjustment Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request an Account Balance Adjustment Report (or a report with a similar name) for each daily adjustment of each ID of the authorised players.

- The name and account number of the authorised player
- The date and time when the account balance was adjusted
- The unique transaction number
- The user ID and the name of the employee who carried out the account balance adjustment transaction, if applicable
- The user ID and the name of the supervisor who authorised the account balance adjustment
- The amount by which the account balance was adjusted
- The account balance before adjustment
- The account balance after adjustment
- The type of account adjustment
- The reason/description of the account balance adjustment

### Promotional Events Summary Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Report summarising the Account-Related Promotional Elements (or a report with a similar name) for each promotion and/or loyalty bonus.

- The ID of the promotion and/or bonus
- The type of promotion and/or bonus
- The start date of the promotion and/or bonus
- The end date of the promotion and/or bonus
- The status of the promotion and/or bonus (e.g. ongoing, ended, etc.)
- The total balance allocated to the promotion and/or bonus
- The total amount of the prizes allocated to the promotion and/or bonus
- The total amount used for the promotion and/or bonus
- The total amount of the adjustment for the promotion and/or bonus
- The final balance of the promotion and/or bonus.

### Software Change Management Report

Remote gaming systems must be able to supply upon request a Software Change Management Report (or a report with a similar name) for all of the software changes made to the organiser's system.

- Previous version
- Current version
- The date when the software change was made
- A description of the software change
- The category of the software change
- The reason for the software change
- Identification of the person who made the change
